  • Araku

    • Coffee was introduced in Eastern Ghats of Andhra Pradesh in 1898 by British in Pamuleru valley in East Godavari district. Subsequently, it spread over to Araku Valley in the early 19th century. After independence, the Andhra Pradesh Forest Department developed coffee plantations in the valley. In 1956, Coffee Board appointed Andhra Pradesh Girijan Cooperative Corporation Limited (GCC) for promoting coffee plantations in the valley through local tribal farmers.[clarification needed] In 1985, the plantations were handed to A.P. Forest Development Corporation and GCC promoted Girijan Coop. Plantation Development Corporation (GCPDC) exclusively to develop coffee plantations in tribal areas. All the plantations developed by GCC and GCPDC were handed over to the tribal farmers at two acres per family.Apiculture farms have been widely spread in Araku, several types of flavoured honey is being made commercially.(Araku is famous for bamboo chicken)

  • Rushikonda Beach

    • Rushikonda Beach in Visakhapatnam, Andhra Pradesh, has a rich history that includes: Name The name Rushikonda translates to "Hill of the Sages" and comes from the Saptarshi Swaralayam temple, built by seven sages on the coast. Sailors In the 17th and 18th centuries, sailors braved the high seas to reach the port cities of Vizagapatam and Bheemunipatam. Romans In the 2nd century BC, Romans came to Thotlakonda in oar-driven galley ships to trade with Buddhist monks. Blue Flag beach In 2020, the International Committee of Blue Flag awarded Rushikonda a certificate after it met the guidelines for a Blue Flag beach. Activities Visitors can enjoy water sports like scuba diving, kayaking, parasailing, speed boating, and jet skiing. Photography The beach is a great place for photography, with lush green hills and verdant landscapes as the backdrop. Hiking The hills surrounding the beach have trails for hiking, with great views of the coastline and the Bay of Bengal.
